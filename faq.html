Still got some questions for us?

Don't worry, we're all about questions. Please contact us if you have more.

What kind of question should I ask?

You can ask whatever you want. We like questions that are deep and meaningful and truly important. Please keep in mind that we prefer if you ask questions that require a YES or NO response and rationale.

For example:

Would there be less war without religion?

This is a good question that requires a YES or NO response and rationale.

If, however, you ask:

What do you think of religion

You're not exactly on the right path of getting a YES or NO response.

What's the point of all of this?

Many people have never ever thought to ask themselves what's the single most important question in the world. Ask Around the World is a catalyst to make people think. Secondly, it's a place to listen and learn about what's important to other people around the planet.

Will someone answer my question?

Nobody will answer every question posted on the site. Ask Around the World is, however, an initiative of AskVote.com, an online platform for asking questions and engaging in intelligent debate. AskVote.com is the next generation of online conversation. Click here to register and be one of the first to take part in this exciting project.

Will my question be published?

We reserve the right to approve or not approve each submission on a case-by-case basis. There's no guarantee that your video will make the cut, so try to ask a good question and try to have fun and be creative.

What charities will the money go to?

For every video we publish, Ask Around the World will donate US$1 to the following global aid organisations: Oxfam, Save the Children and Médecins Sans Frontières.

Ask Around the World will also contact 10 people whose videos we like the best and, on their behalf, donate and additional US$200 each to the charities of their choice.

Do you want creative videos?

We love entries that have lots of personality and that might show interesting things in the background. If you're in Italy you might ask your question with the Colosseum behind you. In southern Africa, it could be an elephant in the frame. It could be with friends from school, university or work. It could involve silly props and funny interpretive dancing. We'd love a deep and meaningful question from a talking bird. Feel free to be serious or to have fun with your short video question (10 seconds or less). Just provide us with a snapshot of where you live.
